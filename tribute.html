Shaheed Bhagat Singh

"They may kill me, but they cannot kill my ideas. They can crush my body, but they will not be able to crush my spirit."
- Bhagat Singh

About the Legend

☛ Born: September 28, 1907, in Banga, Punjab, British India.

☛ Revolutionary Involvement: Joined Hindustan Socialist Republican Association, participated in anti-British activities.

☛ Assassination of Saunders: Plotted and executed the killing of British police officer John Saunders in 1928.

☛ Assembly Bombing: Threw non-lethal bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi in 1929 to protest against repressive laws.

☛Trial and Execution: Arrested, tried for Saunders' murder and assembly bombing, sentenced to death, and hanged on March 23, 1931.

☛ Ideological Influence: Inspired by socialist and Marxist ideologies, advocated for socialist society and rights of the oppressed.

☛Legacy: Revered as a national hero in India, symbol of resistance against British colonial rule.

☛Slogan: Popularized the slogan "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live the Revolution).

☛ Literary Contributions: Wrote articles advocating for independence and social justice.

☛ Youth Icon: Believed in the power of youth to bring about social change, emphasized revolutionary action for independence.
